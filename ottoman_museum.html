History

Six Centuries
of Empire

✦

1299
Founding of the Empire
Osman I establishes the Ottoman dynasty in Söğüt, laying the foundations of what would become one of the longest-lasting empires in history.
1453
Conquest of Constantinople
Sultan Mehmed II captures Constantinople, renaming it Istanbul and transforming Hagia Sophia into an imperial mosque. The Byzantine era ends.
1520
The Magnificent Century
Suleiman the Magnificent ascends the throne, ushering in the peak of Ottoman power, art, law, and architecture.
1683
The Siege of Vienna
The failed siege of Vienna marks the turning point. Ottoman expansion reaches its westernmost limit; the long retreat from Europe begins.
1922
End of the Sultanate
Mehmed VI, the last Sultan, departs Istanbul in exile. The six-century empire comes to its end. The Republic of Turkey is born the following year.
